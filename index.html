Quality, tradition, uniqueness. These are the typical words that can be attached to the style of Persian carpets. Persian carpets have long fascinated people from different cultures and backgrounds. Today, they are still as impressive as they were 2 000 years ago and people all around the globe admire the intricacy of their design and production.



Why are Persian Carpets so fascinating?

Persian carpets have become popular all around the world. Although they come from Persia (Iran), nowadays people from different cultures admire them and get them for their home. But what makes them so desirable and unique? Is it the quality, the design, the culture or all of the above?


Ancient art

The art of carpet weaving began in Iran and can be traced back to 2 500 years ago. The oldest known carpet in the world is Pazyryk carpet, found during 1949 from the grave of a Scythian nobleman. Radiocarbon testing indicated that the Pazyryk carpet was woven in the 5th century BC.

Roots and spreading

The skill and craftsmanship which are involved in the production of Persian carpets have been passed down from generation to generation, through war and peace. After the Persian empire fell apart, areas that no longer belonged to Persia preserved the tradition of carpet-making. Eventually, the art of rugs made it to Romania, India and China.

Materials

The most used materials are wool and cotton. Wool is commonly used because it’s soft, durable and also available natural source in Iran. The best wool for carpet production comes from colder, higher altitudes. Cotton, on the other hand, is used for the foundation of the rugs. However, some weavers prefer cotton for making white details.

Colors and design

The meaning of colors varies across different cultures. In Muslim countries green is sacred, symbolizing 'Mohammed’s coat', and is therefore less used. However, green is often seen in Chinese carpets. The opposite applies to yellow which is seen as the Emperor’s color in China. More universally, red symbolizes power and richness.gfd
